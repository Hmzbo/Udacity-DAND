Ford GoBike System Data Exploration¶

by Hamza Boulahia¶

Investigation Overview¶

The goals of the investigation we made are to draw insights that would help us better understand the workflow of the Ford GoBike System Data. The main questions that we're going to answer are the folowing:

  1. How long does the average trip takes?
  2. When are most trips taken in terms of day of the week?
  3. What are the most frequented stations?
  4. Does the duration of the trips depends on the year or user_type?

Dataset Overview¶

In this dataset exploration project, we analyzed the data of a bike sharing system located in the greater San Francisco Bay area which cover three regions San Francisco, East Bay, and San Jose. In the present dataset we have informations are about 4,890,404 trips taken from the year 2017 to 2019.

all_files = os.listdir()
csv_files = [item for item in all_files if item[len(item)-4:len(item)]=='.csv' ]
li=[]
for filename in csv_files:
    temp_df = pd.read_csv(filename, index_col=None)
    temp_df['year']=str(filename[0:4])
    li.append(temp_df)

df = pd.concat(li, axis=0, ignore_index=True)
drop_list=['rental_access_method','bike_share_for_all_trip','start_station_latitude','start_station_longitude','end_station_latitude','end_station_longitude','bike_id']
df.drop(drop_list, axis=1, inplace=True)
max_dur = df.duration_sec.max()
df.query('duration_sec==@max_dur')
idx = df[df['start_station_id'].isnull()].index
df.drop(idx, inplace=True)

Getting insights about the trips in each day of the week would help the Ford GoBike Comapny to take effective measure to facilitate the flow of the system.

The first plot in the figure below shows the number of trips taken during each day of the week in the last three years. It is clear that the number of trips taken during the weekend days is on average about half the number of trips taken during workdays.

Further investigation, showed that the average duration of trips taken during the weekends is about 50% longer than the average duration of trips taken in the rest of the week.

Insights:

  • The most frequented start station is Bancroft Way at College Ave with more than 85,000 trips starting there during three years. This station is one of four stations that are the closest to the University of California Berkley and many other institutions.
  • In the other hand, the most frequented end station is Folsom St at 3rd St with almost 120,000 trips ending there during three years. This station is in a residential area which suggest that a good number users of this bike sharing system lives near this area, or visit it frequently for any other reason.

It's important to note that more than 3 million trip during these three years are made from and to a none highly frequenty stations which means stations with less than 33,000 trips during this period.

The charts below shows the difference between both types of users in term on their count and the average duration of their trips, and the evolution of the number of customers and subscribers each year.

The number of trips made by both type of users increased each year with an immense increase in the trips made by subscribers from 2017 to 2018, which implies that this system gained fame and reputation during these years.

Moreover, the total number of trips made by subscribers is more than 4 times the number of trips made by customers, whereas the average duration of the trips made by customers is slightly more than two times the average duration made by subscribers.

Insights: This comparaison suggest that the subscribers are more active in the system and take trips more often but for short duration; For example, going to work, to the gym, or to do groceries..etc. While the customers tends to go for trip less often but for longer duration; For example, going for a long ride during weekends, taking a trip to hangout, or simple exercising..etc.
